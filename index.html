Barbara
Peregonczuk

My work is about the things that make us human: quiet and loud emotions like joy, feeling lost or nostalgic, the wonderful contradictory complexity of human nature, and often a quiet criticism of the materialism of the modern world.

For me, painting has always been a family sport. Everyone in my family paints: my mother, my older sister and my younger brother. So it was probably inevitable that I would end up holding a brush too.

As a teenager I discovered portrait drawing, and I never looked back. Faces have always fascinated me. That level of precision it takes to capture a likeness, or to trap a single emotion on paper. It is one of the hardest things you can do, and that is exactly why I love it.

Today I work primarily in acrylic and oil, creating abstract portraits. My style is constantly shifting and evolving, but there is one thread that runs through all of my work: it combines the perfectionism and careful observation of traditional realistic portraiture with raw, unfiltered emotion, expressed through colour, expression and controlled abstract chaos.
